Research Projects

Single Access Point Based Wi-Fi Localization

Owing to the ubiquitous penetration of Wi-Fi in our daily lives, Wi-Fi indoor localization has attracted intensive attentions in the last decade or so. Despite some significant progresses, the high accuracy of existing systems is still achieved at the cost of dense access point (AP) deployment. We design and implement M3 to tackle this challenge with commodity Wi-Fi cards. M3 exploits a multipath-assisted approach: a device can be pinpointed through the combination of azimuths and the relative time of flight (ToF) of light-of-sight signal and reflection signals, eliminating the requirement of knowing the absolute ToF information. The rationale is turning the harmful “multipaths” from foe to friend for single AP localization. Comprehensive experiments demonstrate that M3 outperforms the state-of-the-art systems and achieves an average median localization accuracy of 71 cm in three environments with a single AP.
Publication:
M3: Multipath Assisted Wi-Fi Localization with a Single Access Point
Zhe Chen, Guorong Zhu, Sulei Wang, Jie Xiong, Yuedong Xu, Jin Zhao, Jun Luo, Xin Wang
Submitted to IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC)

CSI Forgery Attacks and Defense in MU-MIMO Networks

In Multiuser MIMO (MU-MIMO), users need to estimate channel state information (CSI) and report it to access points (APs), thus opening a backdoor to attackers who may forge CSI to eavesdrop the content of victims. We explore the eavesdropping attack in a novel and practical context in which CSI forgery entangles MU-MIMO user selection in a many-users regime. We propose new attack and defense mechanisms: (1) USE Attack that enables attackers to achieve near optimal eavesdropping opportunity and high decoding quality; (2) AngleSec that exploits channel reciprocity for attacker detection in which CSI forgery induces a mismatching of downlink and uplink angular spectra at the AP. Extensive experiments manifest that USE Attack significantly improves the overall eavesdropping quality and AngleSec is able to detect CSI forgery attackers.
Publication:
On User Selective Eavesdropping Attacks in MU-MIMO: CSI Forgery and Countermeasure
Sulei Wang, Zhe Chen, Yuedong Xu, Qiben Yan, Chongbin Xu, Xin Wang
Accepted by IEEE Conference on Computer Communications (INFOCOM), 2019

Practical User Selection for MU-MIMO Networks

User selection is one of the most important components for next generation multi-user multiple-input-multiple-output (MU-MIMO) WLANs. However, state-of-the-art approaches neglect the heterogeneity of users in the available bandwidth and the number of antennas, which diminishes their performance considerably. To tackle this challenge, we formulate a novel integer optimization framework and propose a low-complexity branch-and-prune algorithm to search for the near-optimal combinations of user antennas. Extensive experiments show that our algorithm outperforms a benchmark scheme with a 1.18x gain in realistic indoor environments.
Publication:
Practical User Selection with Heterogeneous Bandwidth and Antennas for MU-MIMO WLANs
Sulei Wang, Zhe Chen, Yuedong Xu, Xin Wang, Qingsheng Kong
Accepted by IEEE Wireless Communications Letters

Hybrid Beamforming Based MU-MIMO WLAN System

Massive MU-MIMO is a promising technology to scale network capacity for supporting bandwidth intensive virtual reality applications. The capacity gain grows linearly with the numbers of antennas and users in theory. However, its practical deployment faces three critical challenges in the state-of-the-art WLANs: i) the demand of a large number of expensive RF chains; ii) the linear growth of feedback overheads with the number of antennas; iii) the lack of scalable user selection scheme for a large user population. To address these problems, we design BUSH, a large-scale MU-MIMO prototype that performs scalable beam user selection with hybrid beamforming for phased-array antennas in legacy WLANs. The experimental results show that in terms of total throughput BUSH outperforms the legacy 802.11ac by 2.08x.

BUSH: Empowering Large-Scale MU-MIMO in WLANs With Hybrid Beamforming
Zhe Chen, Xu Zhang, Sulei Wang, Jie Xiong, Yuedong Xu, Xin Wang
